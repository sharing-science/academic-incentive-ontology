With data becoming so important, not just in academia, there has been a push to utilize new technology to maximize dataâ€™s potential and economic ability. Within academia, this is contradicted by the competitiveness of the academic research space. Although academic research is deeply co-operative, researchers also build ideas on top of their rival's as they compete for citations, acceptance, and recognition. This is coupled with a lacking of technological advancements for handling data sharing. Handling data requires accurate metadata such that it is usable even in the passing of hands. We present the Academic Incentive Ontology (AIO) which describes a blockchain-based protocol for data sharing between two researchers. We argue that the nature of blockchain is the perfect environment for trustless and incentivized data sharing. The ontology includes a two-part incentive mechanism designed to confirm citations and reward reproducible research methods.
